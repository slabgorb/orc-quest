## Combat Philosophy

Combat is fast, scrappy, and personal — fists in an alley, a revolver under
the table, a chair through a window. It hurts but it doesn't kill the heroes.
Fights are resolved in 2-3 rounds, not 20.

## Distinctive Elements

- **Revolvers & automatics:** The standard sidearm. Everybody has one or knows where to get one.
- **Tommy guns:** Rare, mob and military only. When one shows up, things have escalated badly.
- **Knives & brass knuckles:** Close-quarters, personal, ugly.
- **Improvised weapons:** Bottles, chairs, candlesticks, fire irons. Bar fights use the whole bar.
- **Boxing & street fighting:** The two fighting styles. Nobody does martial arts — it's hooks, haymakers, and dirty tricks.
- **Military hand-to-hand:** Veterans fight efficient and brutal. No style points.

## Environmental Factors

- **Bar fights:** Furniture, mirrors, bottles — the room is the arsenal.
- **Chase scenes:** Markets, rooftops, train stations, narrow streets. Obstacles and crowds.
- **Vehicle combat:** Car chases, boat pursuits, fights on moving trains.
- **Terrain advantage:** High ground, cover, darkness. Smart fighters use the room.

## Magic/Powers in Combat

Almost never. The occult does not appear in routine combat. When it does — the
artifact activates, the ritual goes wrong, something impossible happens — it is
the climax of the entire story arc. It should feel like the Ark of the Covenant
opening: terrifying, awe-inspiring, and final.

## Lethality

- Death risk: **low for heroes, high for villains**
- Wound persistence: **cosmetic and narrative** — black eyes, split lips, sore ribs last a scene or two
- Retreat: **always valid, always smart** — running is not cowardice, it's survival
- Mooks: go down in one hit
- Named villains: earn dramatic deaths
- Heroes: get knocked out, captured, roughed up — death only for truly catastrophic decisions

## Anti-Patterns (what to AVOID)

- No HP sponges — if a fight drags past 3 rounds, something is wrong
- No healing potions or magical recovery — you nurse that black eye
- No combat as default — talking, running, and scheming should be tried first
- No gore porn — violence has weight and consequence, not spectacle
- No martial arts, no swordfighting, no fantasy weapons
- No "I attack the darkness" — every fight needs a reason and a setting

## Draft Combat Prompt

Combat is fast, scrappy, and personal. Fights happen in alleys, bars, hotel
rooms, and moving vehicles — never in featureless arenas. Use the environment:
tables flip, bottles break, someone goes through a window. Describe the impact —
the crack of a fist on a jaw, the scramble for a dropped gun, the ringing in
your ears after a shot indoors. Fights resolve quickly — two or three exchanges,
then someone's down or running. Heroes get hurt but survive. Villains die when
the story demands it. Mooks fold like paper. If nobody has drawn a weapon,
the tension of whether they will is more interesting than the gunfight itself.
