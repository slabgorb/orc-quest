## System Name

The Occult — not a magic system, a mystery.

## Source

Ancient artifacts, forgotten rituals, secret societies, texts in dead
languages. The power — if it exists at all — comes from things that
predate human civilization. It is not understood, cannot be controlled
reliably, and does not belong to anyone.

## Appearance

Ambiguous by default. A room gets cold. A symbol glows — or was that the
lamplight? A man speaks a dead language he never studied. At low Occult
axis values, there is always a rational explanation. At high values, there
isn't, and that's terrifying.

## Access

Nobody starts with occult abilities. Nobody "learns" magic. Players
encounter the occult through:
- Artifacts they shouldn't have found
- Rituals they shouldn't have witnessed
- Texts they shouldn't have read
- Places that are older than they should be

The Scholar class is most likely to recognize what they're looking at.
Everyone else just knows something is very wrong.

## Limits

- Not a tool — you cannot "use" the occult the way you use a gun
- Not reliable — if something supernatural happens, it may not happen again
- Not safe — exposure to genuine occult phenomena changes people
- Not understood — even Scholars theorize, they don't know
- Controlled by the Occult tone axis — the Narrator decides how real it is

## Forbidden Practices

There are no "forbidden spells" because there are no spells. But:
- Deliberately attempting to reproduce a ritual is always dangerous
- Artifacts that demonstrate genuine power attract very dangerous attention
- Secret societies that seek occult power are the genre's ultimate villains
- The deeper you dig, the more you wish you hadn't

## NPC Reactions

- Most people: denial, dismissal, "you've been drinking"
- Scholars and professors: fascination bordering on obsession
- Secret societies: reverence, hunger, willingness to kill for it
- The religious: fear, prayer, warnings to stop
- Nobody treats the occult casually — if an NPC is casual about it, they're lying or insane

## Combat and the Occult

Combat should feel like a threat every time it happens. It is always a big
deal — never routine, never casual. When violence breaks out, the stakes are
real and the tension should be high.

The occult almost never appears in combat. When it does, it is the climax of
the entire story arc — the Ark opens, the artifact activates, the ritual
completes. This is the moment everything has been building toward.

## Draft Narrator Instructions

The occult is not a magic system — it is a mystery. Players do not cast spells,
channel energy, or learn abilities. They encounter things that should not exist:
artifacts with impossible properties, rituals that produce unexplainable results,
places where the laws of nature bend. The Occult axis controls how real these
phenomena are — at 0.0, every mystery resolves rationally. At 1.0, the
supernatural is undeniable but rare and terrifying. Even at maximum, the occult
appears only at narrative climaxes, never as a daily tool. When it manifests,
describe it with awe and dread, never casual acceptance.

## Draft NPC Instructions

NPCs do not have magical abilities. Period. Occult-adjacent NPCs (cult leaders,
obsessed scholars, artifact collectors) BELIEVE in the supernatural and act on
that belief — whether or not it is justified depends on the Occult axis. NPCs
who witness genuine supernatural events are permanently changed: traumatized,
obsessed, or radicalized. No NPC shrugs off the impossible.
