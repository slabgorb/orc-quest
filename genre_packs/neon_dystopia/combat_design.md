## Combat Philosophy

Combat is chrome-fast and cinematic. Reflex boosters turn firefights into
bullet-time ballets. Cyberblades cut through armor. Smart weapons track
targets around corners. It's beautiful and brutal — and it costs. Chrome
takes damage. Ammo runs out. The ripperdoc bills aren't free.

## Distinctive Elements

- **Firearms:** Smart pistols, assault rifles, shotguns, sniper systems. Smart weapons lock on. Dumb weapons are cheaper and untraceable.
- **Cyberweapons:** Mantis blades, gorilla arms, monowire, projectile launchers built into the body. Your body IS the weapon.
- **Melee:** Katanas, combat knives, electrified batons. Street samurai still prefer a blade.
- **Netrunning in combat:** Quickhacking enemies — disabling optics, overheating chrome, crashing weapons. Net stat attacks.
- **Environmental:** Exploding fuel cells, collapsing infrastructure, hacking environmental controls, using the city's systems against enemies.

## Lethality

- Death risk: **moderate for heroes, high for everyone else**
- Chrome damage: cyberware can be damaged in combat, requiring repair
- Wound persistence: injuries need medical attention, not rest
- Retreat: always smart — the street doesn't reward heroic last stands
- Mooks: go down fast — gangers, corporate security grunts
- Named villains: earn dramatic deaths or dramatic defeats
- Heroes: get hurt, lose chrome, wake up in ripperdoc clinics. Die only for story reasons.

## Anti-Patterns

- No HP sponges — fights are fast and decisive
- No healing potions — you need a medkit or a ripperdoc
- No turn-based grid combat — cinematic descriptions, not tactical puzzles
- No technical hacking puzzles during combat — quickhacks are narrative actions

## Draft Combat Prompt

Combat is chrome-fast and cinematic. Describe the flash of cyberblades, the
whine of smart weapons acquiring targets, the jolt of a quickhack overloading
someone's optics. Use the environment — exploding screens, sparking cables,
collapsing walkways. Fights are decisive — three exchanges and someone is down,
running, or flatlined. Chrome takes damage — describe sparking cyberarms,
glitching optics, servos grinding. Heroes survive but pay the price in repair
bills and recovery time. The street doesn't do heroic last stands — running
is survival, and survival is victory.
