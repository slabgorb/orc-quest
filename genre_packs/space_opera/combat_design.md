# Combat Design — Space Opera

## Combat Philosophy
Combat is fast, dramatic, and crew-driven. Getting shot matters but doesn't
require a spreadsheet. The most dangerous weapon in the room might be
someone's mouth.

## Distinctive Elements

### Personal Combat
- **Blasters/energy weapons** — ranged is default, melee is desperate or dramatic
- **Cover-based** — standing in the open gets you killed, cover and positioning matter
- **Boarding actions** — close-quarters chaos in corridors, zero-g transitions, hull breaches
- **Improvised solutions** — vent the atmosphere, reroute the power, hack the door shut
- **No bullet sponges** — hits are consequential. A firefight is 2-3 exchanges, not 12 rounds

### Ship Combat
- **Crew-driven, not stat-driven** — each crewmember contributes from their station
- **Station roles:** Pilot maneuvers, Engineer manages power/repairs, Officer coordinates,
  Soldier operates weapons, Operative runs electronic warfare, Medic handles casualties
- **Ships have condition tracks, not HP** — shields, hull, engines, weapons degrade narratively
- **Every round is a decision** — not "I attack again," but "do we run, fight, or bluff?"
- **Consequences are visible** — sparks, hull groaning, alarms, crew shouting

### Social Combat
- **Negotiations as combat** — diplomatic scenes can be as tense and structured as firefights
- **Cunning and Influence are weapons** — a well-placed word can end a fight before it starts
- **Reputation matters** — who you are and who knows you changes the combat calculus

## Environmental Factors
- **Vacuum** — hull breach is always one bad hit away. Atmosphere is a resource.
- **Zero-G** — changes everything about movement and cover
- **Station/ship interiors** — tight corridors, bulkheads, airlocks as chokepoints
- **Planetary variety** — gravity, atmosphere, terrain all vary by world
- **Ship systems as terrain** — reroute power, seal bulkheads, trigger fire suppression

## Magic/Powers in Combat
None. `magic_level: none`. Tech can be exotic (personal shields, powered armor,
neural interfaces) but it follows rules and has limitations. No space wizards
unless a world explicitly adds them via the Xeno flexibility slot.

## Lethality
- Death risk: **moderate** — bad decisions kill you, but it's not random
- Wound persistence: **temporary** — med-tech is good, but injuries affect you until treated
- Retreat: **always valid** — running is smart, not cowardly. The ship is right there.
- Armor tiers: unarmored → light (flight suit) → medium (tactical) → heavy (powered)

## Anti-Patterns (what to AVOID)
- **HP sponges** — no enemy that takes 15 hits to drop
- **Meaningless mook waves** — if combat doesn't have stakes, skip it (Cut the Dull Bits)
- **Solo hero combat** — this is a crew game, combat should involve multiple crew roles
- **Detailed ballistics** — we don't simulate muzzle velocity or penetration values
- **Combat as default** — many problems are better solved by talking, running, or cleverness
- **Sound in space** — well, actually... Rule of Cool says some rumble is fine

## Draft Combat Prompt
Combat in space opera is fast, consequential, and crew-driven. Describe the
chaos — alarms, sparks, shouting over comms. Every hit matters: there are no
HP sponges, no 12-round slugfests. A firefight is 2-3 tense exchanges where
cover, positioning, and clever thinking determine the outcome. Ship combat is
a team sport — each crew station contributes and every round is a decision,
not just an attack roll. Retreat is always an option and often the smart one.
If a fight doesn't force a meaningful choice, skip it entirely.
